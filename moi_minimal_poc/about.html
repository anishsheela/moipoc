MOI™ — My Own Information
Your Personal Information Vault

MOI™ is a product of ZK Group, an Authenticity Enterprise. In technical terms it is a personal information sync-and-license product — a super-secure digital file cabinet that puts you in full ownership and control of every piece of information about yourself.

With file sync-and-license, access to any file or data element by anyone other than its owner requires a license issued by the owner. Think of MOI™ as a file cabinet with three drawers.

Key Principle

MOI gives you the tools to claim your personal information as your Personal Intellectual Property, covered by both international copyright law and secrecy law. Anyone who wants to use your information must consent to a Personal NDA and obtain a license from you specifying permissible uses. Unauthorized use entitles you to compensatory and punitive damages.

Legal Framework
Biographical Reference Work & Personal NDA

The contents of the first two drawers together form your Biographical Reference Work (BRW) — a legal construct that applies copyright law to your personal information as a "work," in the same category as films, books, and reference works.

Legal algorithms are as much a part of MOI™ as computer algorithms. In addition to copyright, the BRW is protected by secrecy law. The World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO), a United Nations agency, defines "trade secret" to include "consumer profiles" — which encompasses the MOI owner's personal information.

The BRW qualifies as a copyrightable reference work, covering both field-oriented data (phone numbers, addresses) and unstructured content (images, text). Copyright applies internationally.
🔒
Trade Secret / Secrecy Law
WIPO's definition of "trade secret" explicitly includes "consumer profiles," providing an independent legal layer of protection over and above copyright for your personal information.

Each relying party must obtain a license specifying permissible uses. Any use outside the license terms constitutes theft and entitles you to compensatory and punitive damages.
No-Yes Protocol
Privacy-Preserving Binary Queries

The No-Yes Protocol enables relying parties to query a data item in the MOI owner's vault to determine whether it meets the requirements of a binary question — without ever seeing the underlying data. For example, a gambling site may ask "Is this person 21 or older?" and receive only "Yes" or "No" — never the actual date of birth.

The vault computes the answer locally from attested data. The relying party never sees the underlying record — only the derived boolean. Your date of birth, for example, is never transmitted to or stored by the querying service.

Authentication
Osmio ID Pair

Authentication to the MOI is exclusively by means of an Osmio VRD Credential™ Utility Certificate. The City of Osmio is a global certification authority whose initial charter was written at the Geneva headquarters of the International Telecommunication Union (ITU), a United Nations agency, on March 7, 2005.

Each MOI owner holds an Osmio ID Pair: a Foundation certificate (attesting personal identity) and a Numberplate certificate (a pseudonymous link). Together they authenticate every vault session and back the IDQA (Identity Quality Assurance) score displayed in the dashboard.

Council of Attestation Officers
Osmio Enrollment Procedure

Under Development — The Council of Attestation Officers demo is not yet finalized. The description below reflects the full intended demo experience.

An Attestation Officer is a Remote Online Notary commissioned by the US Commonwealth (state) of Virginia to perform online notarial procedures. The affiant — the person making the declaration — may be anywhere on Earth.

One such procedure is the enrollment of an individual to the Osmio digital identity certificate stack. The Attestation Officer assesses the evidence of identity presented by the affiant and produces an IDQA (Identity Quality Assurance) score — a number reflecting the reliability of the identity claim. This score is embedded in the resulting Osmio certificates and is visible in the MOI vault dashboard.

In the full demo, certain fields in the MOI — such as date of birth — will be digitally signed by the Attestation Officer, meaning they cannot be altered by the MOI owner themselves. For simplicity, this PoC allows demo users to work with pre-attested sample data.

About the City of Osmio

Osmio is a global certification authority. Its initial charter was written at the Geneva headquarters of the International Telecommunication Union (ITU), a United Nations agency, on March 7, 2005. The Osmio Numberplate and Foundation certificates together form the Osmio ID Pair — the sole authentication mechanism for every MOI session.
